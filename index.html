Getting Here

Crocodile Rock River Lodge is nestled within the private Mjejane Game Reserve on the southern banks of the Crocodile River — sharing an unfenced border with Kruger National Park. The drive here is the beginning of your escape. The further you travel from the city, the wilder and quieter the world becomes.

Drive Times

  • From Johannesburg: approximately 4 hours via the N4
  • From Pretoria: approximately 3.5 hours via the N4
  • From Nelspruit / Mbombela: approximately 45 minutes

Entering Mjejane Game Reserve

The lodge is accessed directly through the Mjejane Game Reserve gate. Reserve levies are payable at the entrance gate for all guests in your vehicle — please have cash or card ready on arrival. Staff at the gate will guide you through the reserve to the lodge.

Guests travelling through Kruger National Park can enter via Malelane Gate or Crocodile Bridge Gate, both conveniently close to the reserve. Note that standard Kruger Park entry fees apply at these gates.

GPS Coordinates

Set your navigation to: −25.408520, 31.673786

We recommend saving these coordinates or downloading an offline map before you set off — GPS signal can be intermittent in the bush.

Road Conditions & Vehicle Requirements

The final stretch to the lodge follows a gravel road through the reserve. In the dry season, a standard sedan handles it comfortably. After heavy rains, a high-clearance 4x4 or SUV is strongly recommended. If you're unsure about conditions on the day of your arrival, give us a call and we'll let you know what to expect.

A Note on Game

Please drive slowly and remain alert from the moment you enter the reserve. Animals roam freely — elephants, hippos, and antelope are regular visitors to the road. This is their home, and yours for the days ahead.
